Military Brat: A Journey Down Memory Lane

My father was enlisted in the Air Force throughout my childhood, and I had the opportunity to move around quite a bit as a child.
When people learn this, I get the question,

"So, where all have you lived?"

Well, let me tell you...


1.) Anchorage, AK

Yes, yes, I know. I have been told there was two feet of snow on the ground the day I was born.

I love that I was born in such a cool (pun definitely intended) place. We left Alaska when I was a toddler, but I did get to go back when I was in grade school.
All I really recall from that trip was just how cold and beautiful it was.

Things Alaska is famous for:


The tallest mountain in North America



Inuit and igloos



The Northern Lights



Caribou



2.)Miami, FL

Quite the temperature change, aye?


Living in Miami was amazing. I remember regularly going to Naples Beach as a very small child, and playing outside nearly all of the time.
We also had a screened-in porch on the back of our house where we would watch the milder tropical storms go by.

Things Florida is famous for:


Walt Disney World (and a few other theme parks)



The Everglades National Park



Cape Canaveral



Beaches!



3.) Stuttgart, Germany

We "crossed the pond" for this one! (In this case, "pond" is cool military speak for ocean.)


I loved my time in Germany. Part of me will always consider it home as I spent six of my elementary school years there.
We did so much sightseeing in our years there, and that coupled with living a bus and train ride away from so many different countries instilled in me a deep love of travel.
My father went back to live in Germany while I was in college, and I had the opportunity to visit there five additional times!

4.) St Louis, MO

I know what you're thinking- this is clearly the coolest place I've lived!


I have grown up into adulthood in this area, and I have come to love St. Louis. I have built a home and life here with my family, and I think I finally developed some roots.
If/when we move to a new location (#5!!), I will miss calling this city home.

Things St. Louis is famous for:


The Arch



Food! And not just the greasy stuff shown here, STL can be a "Foodie's" paradise.



Forest Park, and its awesome FREE attractions



Beautiful nature trails




And of course, our up and coming tech scene!
